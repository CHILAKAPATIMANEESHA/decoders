Vegetarian

Vegetarian diets are associated with a reduced risk of chronic illnesses such as heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers. Because it emphasizes whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes, and nuts, vegetarian food tends to be lower in saturated fats and cholesterol. Many studies suggest that vegetarians tend to have a lower body mass index (BMI), lower blood pressure, and longer life expectancy.Vegetarianism is a dietary lifestyle that excludes the consumption of meat, fish, and poultry. Many people adopt a vegetarian diet for various reasons, including health benefits, ethical concerns about animal welfare, environmental impact, or cultural and religious beliefs. A well-balanced vegetarian diet can provide all the essential nutrients the body needs and is often rich in fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants.
